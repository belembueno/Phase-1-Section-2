Toussaint Louverture

Leader of the Haitian Revolution

Life of Toussaint Louverture

  1. c. 1743: Toussaint Louverture is born enslaved on a plantation in Saint-Domingue - present-day Haiti.
  2. 1776: Toussaint gains his freedom.
  3. 1791: There is a massive revolt fueled by brutal conditions.
  4. 1793: France abolishes slavery.
  5. 1794-95: Toussaint joins France's army due to their new stance on slavery and becomes a military leader.
  6. 1796: Toussaint is appointed governor-general of Saint-Domingue.
  7. 1798-99: Toussaint negotiates treaties with the British which secures that they withdraw from Hispaniola.
  8. 1801: Toussaint abolishes slavery in the Spanish colony of Santo Domingo - present-day Dominican Republic. He then declares himself governor-general over the entire island of Hispaniola.
  9. January-May 1802: Napoleon Bonaparte reinstates slavery in French colonies causing tensions to rise again.
  10. June 1802: Napoleon sends a military expedition to Saint-Domingue under General Leclerc to restore full French control.
  11. 1803: Toussaint is captured through trickery and deported to France, where he dies a prisoner in April.
  12. **Note:** January 1, 1804, Haitian forces led by Jean-Jacques Dessalines defeat the French and declare Haiti's independence. This victory is considered a direct result of the foundation laid by Toussaint Louverture.
